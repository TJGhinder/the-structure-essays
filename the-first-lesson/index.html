The First Lesson

If you only remember one thing, let it be this

January 2026 · 4 min read

The capital-T Truth is: There is no capital-T Truth.

This is the central, most important lesson. Be humble, for you are limited in what you can know. There is no capital-T Truth, because as a living, breathing, medium-sized 3-dimensional creature who thinks and moves at medium-speeds through time–you know nothing.

Your full experience of reality is just a small slice of everything out there. You see 0.2% of the colors which exist. You come in contact with 0.01% of the human stories which are shared online every day. If you are an avid reader, the most you can hope to read in your lifetime may be a book a day for 100 years–36,500 books. Out of the millions of pieces of literature humans have written.

You couldn't build a toaster, and certainly not a laptop. You couldn't construct the internet. The minds required to build these tools are the minds made of millions of minds, distributed across generations. Constrained to your tiny slice of spacetime, you know nothing.

To claim to be an arbiter of the secrets of the universe is, objectively, nonsense. There is not a single human alive who understands how the Universe works, or what it even is.

As I have written about previously–stories ultimately build everything, which has meaning to us. There is no single, unassailable story. There just isn't.

If you zoom into molecules and atoms far enough, you see that there is actually nothing there at all–only the possibilities that things might be in particular places, or have particular momentum.

General relativity shows us that different observers can move through time at different rates--what even is 100 years of time?

You want to claim Truth? On such flimsy fabric?

The Universe teaches us to be humble, because for all we can see, we only truly see that we see nothing. As the island of knowledge grows, the perimeter of ignorance grows even larger. The more we know, the more we know we do not know.
